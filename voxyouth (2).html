Advocacy Tactics

Ways to make your voice heard

📋

Start or sign a petition

Petitions show legislators there's community support behind an issue. Even a few hundred signatures from constituents can prompt a real response from an office.

Good first step
🎤

Testify at a public hearing

Local school boards, city councils, and state committees all hold public comment sessions. Three minutes of personal testimony from a student carries enormous weight.

High impact
✉️

Write to your representative

A personal, specific letter beats a form email every time. Describe how an issue affects you directly and what action you'd like them to take.

Accessible
📰

Submit a letter to the editor

Local newspapers still reach decision-makers. An op-ed or letter from a young person on a local issue often gets published and noticed.

Amplifies your reach
🏫

Organize at your school

Start a club, host a forum, or bring a speaker to campus. Building a community of informed peers is its own form of civic power.

Community builder
🗳️

Run a voter registration drive

If you're 17 or turning 18, you can register now in many states. Help eligible classmates register and understand the issues on their local ballot.

Electoral impact
📱

Use social media strategically

Tag your elected officials, share your proposal publicly, or build an audience around a specific issue. Digital pressure is real pressure.

Broad reach
🤝

Meet with your legislator's office

You can request a meeting with a staffer or legislator's office. Come prepared with a one-page brief and a clear ask — offices take these meetings.

Direct access
💡

Connect with VoxYouth

Have an idea you want to turn into a real proposal? Reach out and our student team will work with you to get it in front of the right people.
